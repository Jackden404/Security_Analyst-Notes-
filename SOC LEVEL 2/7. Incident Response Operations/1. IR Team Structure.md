# 2. Tabletop Exercises

## What Are Tabletop Exercises?

Discussion-based sessions walking through simulated incidents. No systems affected — tests processes and decision-making.

## Designing Scenarios

Good scenarios are: based on real incidents, relevant to your environment, progressive with decision points.

## Running Exercises

1. Set the scene 
2. Introduce complications 
3. Ask what participants would do 
4. Discuss alternatives 
5. Document gaps
## Common Scenarios

Ransomware attack, data breach, insider threat, supply chain compromise, cloud breach.

# 3. Evidence Handling

## Chain of Custody

Track every piece of evidence: who collected it, when, where stored, who accessed it, any changes made. Without proper chain of custody, evidence may be inadmissible.

## Digital Evidence Preservation

Create forensic images, 
use write blockers, 
hash files immediately (MD5 + SHA256), 
store originals securely.

## Court-Admissible Evidence

Must be: authentic, reliable, relevant. Chain of custody must be documented.

## Evidence Storage

Encrypted storage, access logs, tamper-evident containers, documented transfers, limited access.

# 4. Legal and Regulatory Considerations

## Breach Notification Laws

GDPR: 72 hours. US: 30-90 days varies by state. HIPAA: 60 days for 500+ individuals. PCI DSS: Immediate to card brands.

## GDPR Requirements

Data minimization, right to erasure considerations, cross-border transfer restrictions, processing documentation.

## Evidence Preservation for Legal

Preserve all relevant evidence, issue legal hold notices, document preservation, work with legal counsel.

## Working with Law Enforcement

When: criminal activity, fraud, IP theft, critical infrastructure attack. How: contact FBI/CISA, provide evidence properly, document interactions.

# 6. Post-Incident Activities
## Root Cause Analysis

Determine why the incident happened: initial access vector, why defenses were bypassed, what gaps allowed success.
## Lessons Learned Meetings

Within 1-2 weeks. Include all team members. Focus on process, not blame. Document improvements.
## Process Improvement

Update detection rules, improve playbooks, enhance monitoring, train team, update policies.
## Metrics and Reporting

Total incidents, MTTD, MTTR, cost, improvements made.
