Detection Engineering is the practice of building, testing, and maintaining detection rules that identify malicious activity in your environment. Think of it as the "software engineering" of security monitoring.

Unlike basic alerting (which just flags anomalies), detection engineering focuses on creating reliable, low-false-positive detections that map to real-world threats.

## Why Detection Engineering Matters

A SOC analyst without good detection rules is like a doctor without diagnostic tools. You might spot obvious problems, but you will miss subtle, advanced threats. Detection engineering gives you the tools to:

1. **Reduce alert fatigue** — Fewer false positives means more time for real investigations

2. **Catch advanced threats** — Behavior-based detections catch what signature-based tools miss

3. **Improve response time** — Better detections mean faster identification and containment

4. **Measure effectiveness** — You can track which detections work and which need tuning

## Detection Maturity Levels

Most organizations follow a maturity model for their detection capabilities:

- **Level 0 — Initial**: Ad-hoc alerts, no formal process

- **Level 1 — Minimal**: Basic signature-based detection, high false positives

- **Level 2 — Procedural**: Documented detection rules, regular tuning

- **Level 3 — Organized**: Detection-as-code, version control, automated testing

- **Level 4 — Quantitative**: Metrics-driven, measurable detection coverage

- **Level 5 — Adaptive**: ML-assisted, threat-intel-driven, continuously improving

Most SOCs operate at Level 1-2. The goal of this module is to get you to Level 3.

## Rule-Based vs Behavior-Based Detection

**Rule-based detection** uses predefined patterns to match known threats:

- Signature matching (antivirus style)

- Threshold alerts (e.g., "more than 10 failed logins in 5 minutes")

- IOC matching (IP addresses, file hashes)

**Behavior-based detection** looks at what is normal and flags deviations:

- User behavior analytics (UBA)

- Entity behavior analytics (EBA)

- Statistical anomaly detection

The best detection strategies combine both approaches. Use rules for known threats and behavior-based detection for unknown threats.
