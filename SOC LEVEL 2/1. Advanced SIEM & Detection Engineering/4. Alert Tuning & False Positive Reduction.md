## The Alert Fatigue Problem

SOC analysts receive hundreds or thousands of alerts daily. Studies show that up to 45% of alerts are false positives. This creates alert fatigue where analysts start ignoring or rushing through alerts.

## Why Tuning Matters

Good tuning transforms a noisy SOC into an efficient one:

- Before tuning: 500 alerts/day, 90% false positives

- After tuning: 50 alerts/day, 20% false positives

## Whitelisting vs Filtering

**Whitelisting**: Completely suppressing alerts from specific sources. Use for known-good systems. Risk: Might suppress real attacks.

**Filtering**: Reducing alert volume without complete suppression. Better than whitelisting because you maintain visibility.

## Threshold Optimization

Setting the right threshold is critical. Start with industry benchmarks, then adjust based on your environment.
## Measuring Success

- False Positive Rate: Target less than 20%

- Mean Time to Triage: Should decrease

- Detection Coverage: Do not sacrifice coverage for noise reduction
