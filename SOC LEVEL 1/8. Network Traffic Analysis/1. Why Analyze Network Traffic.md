Network Traffic Analysis (NTA) is the science of capturing, monitoring, and interpreting data traveling across network communications. While attackers can wipe local logs and disable endpoint agents, network packets traveling across physical wires cannot be spoofed or hidden, making NTA a vital component of any security operations center.

---
## 📈 The Value of Network Visibility

When endpoints are compromised, local forensic artifacts (such as event logs, registry keys, and local files) might be altered or completely deleted.

[!IMPORTANT] **Endpoint Agent Independence:** NTA remains highly valuable when local host controls fail because: **NTA does not require agents, so it sees traffic even if EDR is disabled**. It relies on passive network taps or port mirroring (SPAN) at switch and router levels, providing visibility regardless of whether EDR software has been uninstalled or disabled.

---
## ⚖️ Visibility Benefits vs. Limitations

|Advantage / Benefit|Technical Limitation|
|---|---|
|**Tamper-Proof:** Packets traveling the network cannot be edited by attackers after the fact.|**Storage Constraints:** High-volume networks generate gigabytes per second, making long-term PCAP storage expensive.|
|**Agentless:** Captures traffic from IoT devices, legacy operating systems, and network gear without agents.|**Loss of Content:** Encrypted traffic hides packet payloads, limiting analysis to header metadata.|

### 🔒 The Impact of Modern Encryption

[!WARNING] **Encryption Blindness:** The primary limitation of NTA in modern environments is: **Widespread encryption (TLS 1.3) hides content from inspection**. With TLS 1.3, payload contents are encrypted, meaning investigators must rely on traffic metadata (IP addresses, packet sizes, request timing, SNI names, and JA3 hashes) rather than direct payload parsing.

---
## 🗃️ Case Study: The SolarWinds Supply Chain Attack

In 2020, one of the most sophisticated supply chain attacks in history occurred when the SolarWinds Orion platform was compromised. The malicious software update was signed with valid digital certificates, allowing it to bypass standard application whitelists and antivirus controls.

[!TIP] **SolarWinds Detection:** Network Traffic Analysis succeeded where endpoint security failed: **It identified unusual DNS queries from the Orion server to a newly registered domain**. By analyzing DNS telemetry, analysts observed that the Orion server initiated outbound DNS lookups featuring DGA (Domain Generation Algorithm) subdomains to unknown domains, exposing the command-and-control channel.
