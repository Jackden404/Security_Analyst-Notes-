
Post-Incident Activity is the final, yet most important phase for long-term organizational maturity. Once the immediate crisis is contained and systems are restored, the team must evaluate their performance to optimize future response capabilities.

---
## 📅 The Lessons Learned Meeting: Timing & Focus

The **Lessons Learned Meeting** brings the responders, IT operations, legal representation, and stakeholders together.

[!IMPORTANT] **Timing the Meeting:** This post-mortem meeting should be scheduled: **Within one to two weeks, soon enough that details are fresh but delayed enough for reflection**. Running it immediately after the incident leads to fatigue-driven oversight, while delaying it beyond two weeks leads to degraded memory of timeline details.

[!TIP] **Creating a Safe Culture:** What makes a lessons learned meeting effective rather than counterproductive? **A blameless environment where team members feel safe admitting mistakes and sharing honest observations**. If the meeting turns into a finger-pointing exercise, team members will hide errors, cover up delays, and refuse to disclose process gaps, defeating the purpose of the review.

## 📝 The Post-Incident Report (PIR)

The **Post-Incident Report (PIR)** is the official audit record of the event. It is distributed to executive leadership, compliance auditors, and internal technical teams.

[!NOTE] **Primary Purpose of the PIR:** The main goal of the post-incident report is: **To document what happened, evaluate the response, and provide specific recommendations to prevent recurrence**.

### Core Sections of a PIR:

- **Executive Summary:** A high-level, business-impact overview for executives.

- **Detailed Incident Timeline:** A minute-by-minute log of detections, containment steps, and recovery actions.

- **Root Cause Analysis (RCA):** The technical explanation of how the attacker breached the network.

- **Efficacy Review:** What worked well (e.g., EDR isolated host within 5 mins) and what failed (e.g., outdated escalation contacts).

- **Remediation Action Items:** A structured list of security fixes, each assigned a specific owner, priority level, and deadline.

## 📊 Key Incident Response Metrics

Organizations track core KPIs over time to measure their incident response maturity:


```
[ Compromise ] ─────── MTTD ───────► [ Detection ] ─────── MTTC ───────► [ Containment ] ─────── MTTR ───────► [ Recovery ]
```

- **MTTD (Mean Time to Detect):** The average duration from initial attacker entry to detection.

- **MTTC (Mean Time to Contain):** The average duration from alert detection to complete host/network isolation.

- **MTTR (Mean Time to Recover):** The average duration from containment to normal production restoration.

- **False Positive Rate:** The percentage of security alerts that did not represent real attacks.
