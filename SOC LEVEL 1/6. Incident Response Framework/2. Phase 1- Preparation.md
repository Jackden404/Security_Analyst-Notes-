Preparation is the foundational phase of the incident response lifecycle. Without pre-established policies, technical playbooks, specialized tools, and trained personnel, an organization's response to an active threat will be chaotic and ineffective.

---
## 📖 The Role of Incident Response Playbooks

An **IR Playbook** is a detailed, technical document detailing the exact actions to take when a specific category of threat (e.g., Ransomware, Phishing, data theft, DDoS) is identified.

[!IMPORTANT] **Purpose of a Playbook:** A playbook exists: **To provide step-by-step procedures for handling specific incident types so the team responds consistently under pressure**. They map out clear escalation paths, key commands, tool inputs, and decision gates, ensuring that responders do not make critical design or authorization mistakes during a high-stress incident.

### Playbook Access and Redundancy

Where you store your playbooks is as important as what is written inside them.

[!CAUTION] **Out-of-Band Access:** You must store playbooks in a location accessible even when the corporate network is down (e.g., printed hardcopies, offline encrypted tablets) because: **Sophisticated attacks like ransomware may encrypt or disable network-dependent systems including file shares and SIEM consoles**.

## 🧰 The Incident Response Kit (IR Kit)

An effective IR kit must be prepared and tested in advance. It includes tools across several categories:

|Tool Category|Common Utilities|Operational Purpose|
|---|---|---|
|**Forensic Acquisition**|`FTK Imager`, `dd`, `Volatility`|Capturing disk images, memory/RAM dumps, and system registry states without contamination.|
|**Network Sniffing**|`Wireshark`, `tcpdump`|Capturing raw packet captures (PCAPs) to identify active C2 connections.|
|**Out-of-Band Comms**|Signal, cellular hotspots, separate Slack|Communication channels that bypass compromised corporate email servers.|
|**Evidence Custody**|Chain of Custody forms, physical evidence bags|Ensuring files and assets are logged to stand up in court.|

## 🎭 Tabletop Exercises: Simulating the Breach

A **Tabletop Exercise** is a facilitator-led simulation where the CSIRT, IT leadership, legal counsel, and executive teams walk through a detailed, evolving attack scenario.

```
[ Phase 1: Attack Detonation ] ──► [ Phase 2: Complications Added ] ──► [ Phase 3: Blameless Debrief ]
  • EDR warns of ransomware          • Backup servers are encrypted        • Review response bottlenecks
  • Mass file renaming begins        • CISO is unreachable                 • Update playbooks
```

[!TIP] **Tabletop Cadence:** Organizations should run tabletop exercises: **Quarterly, because regular practice builds muscle memory and reveals gaps in procedures before real incidents expose them**.

## 📋 The IR Preparation Checklist

-  **IR Policy:** Documented and approved by senior management.

-  **Contact Matrix:** Primary and backup contacts for all CSIRT roles (including legal and external PR).

-  **Playbooks:** Written for the top 5 threat vectors (Phishing, Ransomware, DDoS, Insider, Data Exfiltration).

-  **Out-of-Band Communications:** Separate Slack instance, Signal groups, or offline bridge lines established.

-  **Backups:** Validated quarterly via test restoration.

-  **Forensic Kit:** Dedicated offline acquisition laptop and write-blockers ready.

-  **Retainer Agreement:** Pre-signed contract with an external Incident Response firm.
