In a modern SOC, detection is only half the battle. Once an alert triggers, analysts must investigate, document, and remediate. **SOAR (Security Orchestration, Automation, and Response)** platforms bridge the gap between detection and action, connecting security tools via APIs to automate workflows.

---
## 🧩 What is SOAR?

[!IMPORTANT] **Key Definition:** SOAR stands for **Security Orchestration, Automation, and Response**. It acts as the central coordinator of the security stack, orchestrating actions across firewalls, EDR agents, email gateways, and active directory systems.

---
## 🏆 Leading SOAR Platforms

Here is how the leading enterprise and open-source SOAR solutions compare:

|Platform|Strengths|Weaknesses|Best For|
|---|---|---|---|
|**Splunk SOAR** _(formerly Phantom)_|Deep Splunk ecosystem integration, 300+ pre-built apps, visual playbook builder.|Expensive, per-action licensing, steep learning curve.|Organizations heavily invested in Splunk.|
|**Palo Alto XSOAR** _(formerly Demisto)_|Playbook flexibility (visual & code), **War Room** collaboration, 700+ integrations.|Can be complex to configure, pricing scales with integrations.|Mid-to-large SOCs needing advanced custom playbooks.|
|**TheHive & Cortex**|Completely free, open source, centralized case management, customizable analysis.|Requires manual setup/hosting, no visual playbooks, no official vendor support.|Budget-conscious teams wanting full database/data control.|

---
## ⚔️ Enterprise Deep Dive: Palo Alto XSOAR

Palo Alto's XSOAR is highly regarded for its customizable playbooks and interactive investigation features.

[!TIP] **The XSOAR War Room:** The primary advantage of the Palo Alto XSOAR War Room feature is that **it provides real-time collaboration during active incidents**. Instead of using fragmented communication channels (like Slack, email, or tickets), analysts can document evidence, run CLI commands directly, and collaborate within a single, secure environment.

---
## 🔓 Open Source Alternative: TheHive & Cortex

For teams looking for full data control without vendor lock-in, the combination of **TheHive** and **Cortex** is the gold standard.

[!WARNING] **Open Source Automation:** The SOAR platform combination that is **completely free and open source** is **TheHive and Cortex**.

- **TheHive** functions as a highly collaborative security incident response platform (case management).

- **Cortex** serves as an analysis engine, allowing analysts to run automated queries against VirusTotal, AbuseIPDB, and other threat intelligence feeds.
