Here is a number that should alarm you: the average SOC receives over 11,000 alerts per day. A typical SOC analyst can investigate 5 to 15 alerts per shift. That means even with a team of ten analysts working around the clock, you are investigating less than 10% of your alerts.
## The Alert Volume Problem

A realistic picture of alert volumes in a mid-size enterprise:

- **Perimeter firewall:** 2-5 million events per day, generating 200-500 alerts.

- **Endpoint detection (EDR):** 50,000-200,000 telemetry events per day, generating 100-300 alerts.

- **Email gateway:** 10,000-50,000 emails per day, generating 50-100 alerts.

- **SIEM correlation rules:** 50-200 alerts per day.

- **Vulnerability scanner:** Weekly scans generating 500-2,000 findings.

A single analyst working a 12-hour shift with a 10-minute average investigation time can investigate 72 alerts. Your SOC is generating 400-1,100 alerts per day. The math does not work.
## Analyst Burnout

When an analyst spends eight hours clicking through the same triage workflow for the 500th time, with 499 being false positives, they stop thinking critically. They start clicking "Close - False Positive" without investigating. They miss the one alert that was real. This is how breaches happen.

A Ponemon Institute study found that 65% of security analysts experience burnout. The primary driver is repetitive, low-value work. Automation takes the repetitive work off their plate.
## ROI of Automation

- **Time savings.** If automation saves each analyst 2 hours per day across a 10-analyst team, that is roughly 2.5 FTEs or $225,000 per year in recovered capacity.

- **Faster detection.** Automated correlation detects multi-stage attacks in seconds.

- **Consistency.** An automated workflow performs the same steps every time.

- **Scalability.** When alert volume doubles, you scale automation, not headcount.
## Real Automation Examples

**Example 1: Phishing triage.** Automation extracts sender domain, checks threat intel feeds, detonates attachments in a sandbox, and classifies the email. Malicious emails are auto-removed. Suspicious emails are escalated with full enrichment.

**Example 2: Malware containment.** Automation checks process hash against VirusTotal. If confirmed malicious by multiple engines, it auto-isolates the host and creates a high-priority ticket.

**Example 3: Impossible travel.** Automation pulls user identity from AD, checks VIP status, reviews login history, compares device fingerprints, and checks breach databases.
