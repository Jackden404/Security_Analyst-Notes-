Threat hunting is the proactive, analyst-led search for undetected threats inside an organization's network. It assumes that attackers are already present inside the environment, sailing past signature-based and automated detection rules.

---
## ⚖️ Proactive Hunting vs. Reactive Triage

Most Security Operations Centers (SOCs) spend the majority of their cycles operating in a reactive state.

[!IMPORTANT] **Proactive vs. Reactive Security:** The fundamental operational difference between normal SOC triage and threat hunting is: **Reactive work responds to alerts; hunting proactively searches for undetected threats**.

Reactive teams wait for an alert (EDR warning, firewall notification) before initiating an incident playbook. Threat hunters, however, act under the assumption of compromise, actively searching system logs to locate hidden adversary presence.

---
## 📈 The Hunting Maturity Model (HMM)

The Hunting Maturity Model measures an organization's proactive detection capabilities on a scale of 0 to 4:

- **Level 0 (Initial):** No active hunting. The team only triages automated alerts.

- **Level 1 (Minimal):** Relies on basic threat intelligence indicators (IoCs) to search logs occasionally.

- **Level 2 (Procedural):** Repeats structured, pre-defined procedures from hunting playbooks.

- **Level 3 (Innovative):**

[!NOTE] **HMM Level 3 Characteristic:** At HMM Level 3: **Hunters form their own hypotheses based on adversary TTPs and environmental context**. They understand system baselines and adapt their searches dynamically based on active threat intelligence and attacker behaviors (TTPs).

- **Level 4 (Leading):** High automation. Successful hunts are converted into automated, continuous SIEM detection rules.

---
## 📊 Threat Hunting Success Metrics

To prove the return on investment (ROI) of dedicated threat hunting time, SOC managers track specific performance metrics:

[!TIP] **Key Hunting KPI:** A primary metric used to demonstrate threat hunting success is: **Dwell time reduction compared to previous quarter**.

Dwell time is the duration between initial compromise and detection. Successful threat hunting programs actively locate attackers earlier in the intrusion lifecycle, reducing overall dwell time and limiting the potential blast radius.

### Other Important KPIs:

- **False Negative Discoveries:** Counting how many threats bypassed automated firewalls and EDR rules but were caught during manual hunts.

- **Mitre ATT&CK Coverage:** Tracking how many attack techniques have been validated and mapped to active detection rules.
