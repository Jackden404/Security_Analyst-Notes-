A threat hunt's success is determined by the quality of its hypothesis. A vague question leads to endless query loops and analyst burnout, whereas a specific, testable hypothesis points directly to relevant data sources.

---
## 🎯 Anatomy of an Effective Hypothesis

A structured, actionable threat hunting hypothesis must meet four core criteria:

[!IMPORTANT] **Hypothesis Criteria:** To conduct a successful hunt, your hypothesis must be: **It is specific, testable, grounded in reality, and scoped**.

- **Specific:** Identifies the precise tool, technique, or DLL hijack you are looking for.

- **Testable:** Formulated in a way that you can query active logs (SIEM/EDR) to prove or disprove it.

- **Grounded:** Based on real threat intelligence feeds, incident lessons learned, or adversary write-ups.

- **Scoped:** Targets a specific network segment, list of endpoints, or timeframe (e.g., AD servers over the last 14 days).

---
## 🗺️ Using MITRE ATT&CK as a Hypothesis Engine

The MITRE ATT&CK matrix provides a comprehensive, structured repository of real-world adversary tactics and techniques.

[!TIP] **The Best Source for Hunting Playbooks:** The primary industry framework used to generate structured threat hunting hypotheses is: **MITRE ATT&CK**.

By taking a specific technique from the matrix (e.g., _T1218.011 - Signed Binary Proxy Execution: Rundll32_), hunters can build a dedicated query playbook:

- **Tactic:** Defense Evasion.

- **Technique:** Executing command lines calling `rundll32.exe`.

- **Hypothesis:** "An attacker is using `rundll32.exe` to load DLL payloads from user-writable directories (like `%TEMP%` or `%APPDATA%`)."

---
## ⚠️ Bad Hypotheses to Avoid

Hunts fail when they lack boundary parameters or target abstract goals.

[!WARNING] **Example of a Poor Hypothesis:** A classic example of an ineffective, unusable hunting hypothesis is: **Something bad might be happening**.

This statement cannot be mapped to any log queries, provides no scope parameters, and cannot be disproven or proven, wasting precious analyst hours.

### Good vs. Bad Hypothesis Comparison:

- ❌ **Bad:** "Let's search the firewall logs for hackers."

- 🟢 **Good:** "An adversary might be bypassing our proxy by establishing an SSH tunnel over port 443. I will query network logs for outbound TCP port 443 connections to external IPs that exhibit unusually long session durations."
