If you cannot measure it, you cannot improve it. Security Operations Center (SOC) metrics and Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) demonstrate the operational value of the security team, justify budget and headcount increases, and expose gaps in coverage before they are exploited.

---
## 📈 Core SOC Metrics

```
[ Incident Occurs ] ──► ( MTTD ) ──► [ Alert Detected ] ──► ( MTTR ) ──► [ Incident Contained ]
```

[!IMPORTANT] **Mean Time to Detect:** MTTD measures **Mean Time to Detect - how long between an event occurring and your SOC detecting it**. Keeping this window as small as possible is the primary way to prevent adversaries from establishing deep lateral persistence in your infrastructure.

- **Mean Time to Respond (MTTR):** The average duration between alert detection and complete threat containment. Typical targets are under 30 minutes for critical threats.

- **SLA Compliance:** The percentage of security incidents triaged and resolved within the timeframe agreed upon in internal or external SLA contracts.

---
## 🔇 Alert Tuning & Noise Reduction

A high volume of alerts does not mean your security team is doing a good job; it often points to poorly tuned detection rules that cause analyst fatigue.

[!WARNING] **High False Positive Rates:** A false positive rate of 85% indicates that **your detection rules need tuning to reduce noise**. When 8.5 out of 10 alerts are benign, analysts stop trusting the alert source. Over time, this alert fatigue leads to analysts closing real malicious events without full analysis.

---
## 📊 Trending Analysis vs. Snapshot Reporting

When reporting metrics to leadership or looking to improve internal workflows, static numbers provide little value.

[!TIP] **Trending Over Time:** SOC metrics should be tracked as trends over time rather than snapshots because **a single data point lacks context; trends reveal whether security posture is improving or degrading**. A sudden MTTR spike in a single week could be caused by a single high-impact event (like a zero-day containment). A trend showing MTTR rising steadily over three months indicates a staffing or process bottleneck.

---
## 🎛️ Anatomy of a Mature SOC Dashboard

1. **Operational Health (Real-Time):** Current alert queue depth, active analyst utilization, open incident counts by severity.

2. **Historical Trends (30-Day Rollup):** Historical trends of MTTD, MTTR, and False Positive percentages.

3. **Security Posture (Coverage):** Current MITRE ATT&CK technique coverage mapped against log ingestion states.
